Settlement EV Calculations

Below are the key calculations from the Expected Value formula used in the Submit Deal tab:

Key Constants in EV Calculations

  • Total Hydra Vehicles: 1,000,000
  • Vehicles with Defective Pawls: 100,000
  • Vehicles with Reported Accidents: 111,100
  • Recall Cost per Defective Vehicle: $140 ($40 parts + $100 labor)
  • Recall Cost per Functional Vehicle: $30 (inspection only)
  • Loaner Vehicle Cost: $50 per vehicle per day
  • Court Cost if Going to Trial: $900M
  • PR Damage Cost: $100M
  • Third-Party Evaluation Cost: $500 per claim

EV Calculation Formulas

Settlement Scenario:

  • Recall Costs = (Total - Defective) × Functional Cost + Defective × Defective Cost
  • Loaner Costs = Defective × Daily Cost × Days
  • Compensation Costs = (Defective × Per Owner) + (Accidents × Per Accident)
  • Third-Party Evaluator Impact:
    • Cost = Accident Claims × $500
    • Reduces Accident Claims by 80%
  • PR Damage Cost varies by statement type (% compared to $100M if go to court):
    • With apology and favorable comment: 10% of cost (best to take responsibility and be embraced)
    • With apology, no favorable comment: 70% of cost (worst to take responsibility and be lambasted)
    • Without apology: 50% of cost (middling to ignore responsibility)
    • NOTE: This is based off studies that show apologies affect stock positively if receive positive media attention, and negatively if little or negative media attention
  • Lawyer gets 30% of monetary compensation
  • Plaintiffs get 70% of monetary compensation

No Settlement (Trial) Scenario:

  • Payne Motors EV = -Total Litigation Cost ($900M) - PR Damage ($100M) = -$1B
  • Lawyer's EV = Winnings ($800M) × 30% fee × 75% Win Probability = $180M
  • Plaintiffs' EV = Winnings ($800M) × 70% × 75% Win Probability = $420M

Settlement Incentive Logic

The system checks whether each party would prefer the deal or going to trial:

  • Lawyer prefers settlement if: Settlement EV > Trial EV (Court × 30% × 75%)
  • Payne Motors prefers settlement if: Settlement EV > Trial EV ($900M + $100M PR damage)
  • When both prefer settlement: Green "Both parties prefer to settle"
  • When either prefers trial: Orange/Red warning

Negotiation Tactics

Shell Tactics

  • Strategic Preparation: Complete value analysis and research on Davis Parker's past cases
  • Crafting Powerful Values: Focus on customer safety while maintaining financial viability
  • Shaping Perception: Present ourselves as committed to doing right by customers

Voss Tactics

  • Tactical Empathy: Acknowledge the legitimacy of customer concerns
  • Calibrated Questions: "How would a high-profile trial help your clients?"
  • "That's Right" Moment: Get Davis to acknowledge the benefits of quick resolution

Cialdini Principles

  • Reciprocity: Offer immediate recall initiation before discussing compensation
  • Commitment & Consistency: Get incremental agreements on smaller issues
  • Social Proof: Highlight other successful class-action settlements
  • Scarcity: Time-limited settlement offer with fixed compensation pool

Anchor and Concessions Plan

  1. Initial anchor: Immediate recall + $50M compensation fund for verified damages
  2. First concession: Add loaner vehicles for highest-risk vehicles
  3. Second concession: Increase compensation to $150M with tiered distribution
  4. Third concession: Public statement acknowledging issue (not a full apology)
  5. Final position: $300M compensation + full recall with loaners + public statement
